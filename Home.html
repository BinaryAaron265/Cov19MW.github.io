How Vaccines Work

------------------------------------------------------

------------------------------------------------------

Vaccines help prevent infectious diseases by training the immune system to recognize and fight off specific pathogens, such as viruses or bacteria.

Most vaccines contain weakened or inactivated versions of the pathogen, or pieces of the pathogen, that can't cause disease but still trigger an immune response. Some vaccines, such as mRNA vaccines, contain genetic material that instructs cells to produce harmless pieces of the pathogen.

When a person receives a vaccine, their immune system responds by producing antibodies and other immune cells that can recognize and attack the specific pathogen in the future. If the person is later exposed to the pathogen, their immune system can quickly mount a defense and prevent or reduce the severity of the disease.
